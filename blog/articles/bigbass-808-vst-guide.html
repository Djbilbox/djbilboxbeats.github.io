The 6 modes explained

Each mode reshapes the character of the 808 so you can match it to the track without stacking extra plugins:

SUB — clean, deep low end. For minimal, spacious beats.
SLAM — punchy attack. Cuts through busy mixes.
PUNCH — tight and controlled. Great for drill.
GROWL — added mid grit. Aggressive, modern.
SCREAM — distorted and loud. Rage / hyper-trap.
DOOM — dark, heavy sub-weight. Cinematic and menacing.

How to get a pro 808 with BIGBASS

  • Tune to your key. Match the 808 root to your melody's key — an out-of-tune 808 is the #1 reason a beat sounds off.
  • Use slides. Glide between notes for that signature trap movement, especially on notes leading into the next bar.
  • Pick a mode per genre. SUB/PUNCH for clean drill, GROWL/SCREAM for aggressive trap, DOOM for dark cinematic beats.
  • Let the kick punch first. Sidechain the 808 to the kick, or time them so the kick transient hits before the 808 sustains.
